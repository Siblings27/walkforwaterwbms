Background Info

South Sudan is a country in Central Africa and its capital is Juba. It is the most recent country to gain independence. It has more than 60 ethnic groups and over 60 indigenous languages. It has been through two civil wars while it was part of Sudan. It has a population of nearly 12 million people. Major challenges include poverty, lack of education, government instability, corruption, and most importantly limited access to clean water.

Problem

Unequal access to water causes waterborne diseases such as cholera, typhoid, and dysentery due to poor sanitation. This affects education, especially for girls, lowers literacy rates, weakens the economy, and creates an endless cycle that must be broken.

Solution

For the past 4 years, we have partnered with Water is Basic to repair wells in South Sudan. Our goal is to provide access to clean water by 2030. This year, we aim to raise $15,000 to help 30 villages.

What You Can Do

You can help by donating and spreading awareness. Every contribution helps bring clean water to communities in need.
